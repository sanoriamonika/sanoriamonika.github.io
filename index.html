Monika Sanoria, PhD

Post-Doctoral Scholar • Active Matter & Biophysics

Center for Cellular and Biomolecular Machines, UC Merced

I am a researcher in active matter and biophysics, exploring how self-propelled particles and cells organize, deform, and move collectively in complex environments. My work bridges physics and biology, combining computational modeling with theoretical analysis to uncover fundamental principles governing active systems - from synthetic colloidal particles to cancer cell clusters. I focus on phenomena such as motility-induced phase separation, percolation transitions, and collective chemotactic migration. Through my research, I aim to reveal how simple interaction rules and feedback mechanisms give rise to emergent collective behaviors, with implications for understanding biological processes like cancer metastasis and designing novel active materials.
