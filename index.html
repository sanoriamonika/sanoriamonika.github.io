Monika Sanoria, PhD

Post-Doctoral Scholar • Active Matter & Biophysics

Center for Cellular and Biomolecular Machines, UC Merced

I am a researcher in active matter and biophysics, exploring how particles and cells move, interact, and self-organize in complex environments. My work lies at the intersection of physics and biology, focusing on phenomena such as motility-induced phase separation, percolation transitions, and collective cell migration. Through computational modeling and theoretical analysis, I aim to uncover the fundamental principles governing active systems—from artificial particles to cancer cell clusters. My research enhances our understanding of self-organization in both physical and biological systems, shedding light on emergent behaviors in diverse contexts.
