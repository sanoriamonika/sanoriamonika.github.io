2. Ethical Media Usage

This blog is public, educational, and non-commercial. That helps — but "educational" alone is not a free pass on copyright. Follow these guidelines for every post.

Safe vs risky sources

Source typeStatusWhat to do
Wikimedia Commons (CC-licensed) ✓ Safe Use freely. Always note the specific license (CC BY, CC BY-SA, etc.) and link to the source.
Open-access papers (CC BY) ✓ Safe Embed figures freely. Cite authors, journal, year, DOI, and license.
NIH / PubMed Central open-access ✓ Usually safe Most are CC BY. Verify the license on the paper page before using.
Your own figures / simulations ✓ Safe No restrictions. Still caption clearly.
bioRxiv preprints ✓ Usually safe Most preprints are CC BY. Use the preprint figure + cite as preprint. Safer than journal version.
YouTube / Vimeo embeds ✓ Safe Embedding is permitted by platform ToS. Credit channel name and link to video.
Twitter / LinkedIn embeds ✓ Safe Platform-sanctioned embedding. Attribution is visible in the embed itself.
Paywalled journal figures (non-Elsevier) ⚠ Gray area Fair use likely applies when discussing the exact paper. Always link to DOI, cite fully, add fair use note in caption. Prefer to link rather than embed if unsure.
Hotlinking from journal servers ⚠ Avoid Uses their bandwidth. URLs break when journals change servers. Download and re-host, or link instead.
Elsevier journal figures ✗ Risky Elsevier is the most aggressive publisher on copyright. Safer to describe the figure in words and link to it. See note below.
Full paper copies / PDFs ✗ Do not host Never host full PDFs of papers you don't own. Link to the DOI instead.
bioRxiv first rule: Before embedding a journal figure, check if the paper has a preprint on bioRxiv. If it does, use the preprint figure — it's almost always CC BY and legally safer, even if the journal version is paywalled. The figures are usually identical.

Using a figure when discussing the same paper

This is your strongest fair use case. When you are summarizing or commenting on a specific paper, using one of its figures works in your favor on all four fair use factors:

Fair use factorYour situation
PurposeEducational, non-commercial, direct commentary on the work ✓
Nature of the workScientific / factual — easier to use than creative fiction ✓
Amount usedOne figure from a multi-figure paper ✓
Market effectYou drive readers to the paper — not replacing it ✓

What strengthens the case further:

  • Always include the DOI link
  • Credit authors, journal, year, and figure number
  • Add your own analysis — don't just re-caption their figure
  • If open-access, note the license explicitly

Elsevier note

Elsevier is the exception. They have sued Sci-Hub ($15M, 2017), sent DMCA takedowns to ResearchGate, and even sent takedowns to researchers hosting their own papers on personal websites. While they are unlikely to pursue a small educational blog, the risk is real enough to avoid. For papers published in Elsevier journals (Cell Press, Biophysical Journal, Current Biology, Physica A, Journal of Theoretical Biology, etc.):
  • Describe the figure in your own words rather than embedding it
  • Link directly to the figure on the journal's website
  • Check if the paper has a bioRxiv preprint and use that figure instead

Main Elsevier journals in your area (Active Matter / Biophysics):

  • Cell, Current Biology, Developmental Cell, Biophysical Journal, Cell Reports — Cell Press (owned by Elsevier)
  • Physica A, Journal of Theoretical Biology, Progress in Biophysics and Molecular Biology

Safer alternatives in your field: Physical Review journals (APS), eLife (CC BY), PLOS Biology (CC BY), Nature/Science (not Elsevier), bioRxiv preprints.

Citation format in captions

Use the author, url, and license parameters in the includes. The rendered caption will look like:

Figure 1: Actin filament organization during collective migration.
— Smith et al., Nature 2023 (CC BY 4.0)

Figure 2: Schematic of self-propelled particle model.
— Jones & Lee, PRX Life 2024 (CC BY 4.0)

Figure 3: Wound-healing assay showing leader cells.
— Chen et al., Biophysical Journal 2022. Used for commentary under fair use.
All rights remain with the authors and Elsevier.
For Elsevier specifically, replace the license parameter with: license="Used for commentary under fair use. Rights remain with authors/Elsevier."
