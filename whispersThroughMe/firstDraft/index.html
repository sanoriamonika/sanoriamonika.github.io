On the Nature of Questions

Existence November 05, 2025

There’s something profound about sitting with a question, letting it breathe, not rushing to answer it. We’re taught to solve, to conclude, to wrap things neatly. But what if the question itself is the gift?

When I ask “What is my purpose?” I’m really asking “Who am I becoming?” The question shifts, morphs, reveals new facets depending on when I ask it, how I ask it, why I ask it.

Maybe we’re not meant to answer everything. Maybe we’re meant to live the questions.

“Be patient toward all that is unsolved in your heart and try to love the questions themselves.” — Rilke knew something about this.

The universe doesn’t provide answers on demand. It provides experiences, moments, silences that gradually reshape our questions into something we can live with—something that lives with us.

Today, I’m learning to be comfortable not knowing. And perhaps that’s a kind of knowing in itself.
