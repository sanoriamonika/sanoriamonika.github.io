Summary

I am a researcher in active matter and biophysics, exploring how particles and cells move, interact, and self-organize in complex environments. My work focuses on MIPS, percolation transitions, and collective cell migration, using computational modeling and theoretical analysis to uncover principles governing active systems.

Research Experience

  • Post-Doctoral Scholar, UC Merced — Center for Cellular & Biomolecular Machines
    Feb 2023–Present

    Advisor: Prof. Ajay Gopinathan

    • Project: "Chemotactic Migration of Multicellular Clusters" — Investigating how multicellular clusters migrate in chemical gradients using computational and theoretical approaches.
    • Focus on chemorepulsion at high gradient strengths, where single cells are repelled yet clusters migrate up the gradient.
    • Exploring collective dynamics, shape changes, stability, and the physical principles behind efficient chemotactic migration.
  • Research Scholar, IIT Bombay
    Sep 2022–Jan 2023

    Advisors: Prof. Amitabha Nandi and Prof. Raghunath Chelakkot

    • Project: "Percolation transition in binary active particles"
    • Examined the influence of softness on binary active particles using Brownian dynamics simulations.
  • PhD Researcher, IIT Bombay
    Jan 2017–2023

    Advisors: Prof. Amitabha Nandi and Prof. Raghunath Chelakkot

    • Examined the influence of softness on emergent phenomena using Brownian dynamics simulations and statistical approaches.
    • Demonstrated several new transitions in active Brownian systems using multiple statistical approaches and structure analyses.
  • Junior Research Fellow, IISER Mohali
    Dec 2015–Dec 2016

    Advisor: Prof. Dipanjan Chakraborty

    • Project: "Investigating the self-propulsive mechanisms of thermally driven Janus particles"
    • Used Brownian particle dynamics for simulating NPT and NVT ensembles using molecular dynamics simulations and GPU CUDA parallel programming.
